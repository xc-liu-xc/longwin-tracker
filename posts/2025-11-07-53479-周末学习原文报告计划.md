Source: Finaeon, Deutsche Bank. Note: Germany included twice to account for the post hyperinflation period.

2）原文阅读

Introduction

引言

Almost everyone reading this has a reason to invest for the long term — whether that’s building a pension, planning for early retirement, funding a child’s education, helping them buy a property, or simply setting something aside for a rainy day. At a professional level, understanding how financial assets behave over long horizons is fundamental to strategic asset allocation, long-term investing, and economic forecasting.

几乎每一位阅读本篇研究的人都有长期投资的需求 —— 无论是积累养老金、规划提前退休、为子女教育出资、助力他们购置房产，还是单纯储备应急资金。从专业角度而言，理解金融资产在长期维度的表现，是战略资产配置、长期投资及经济预测的核心基础。

This study examines how asset classes have performed across a wide range of macroeconomic, policy, and valuation environments. Drawing on data that in some cases stretches back to the 18th century, we analyse both nominal and real returns to understand how different assets have behaved under varying conditions.

本研究探讨了各类资产在不同宏观经济、政策及估值环境下的表现。我们采用的部分数据可追溯至 18 世纪，通过分析名义回报与实际回报，旨在厘清不同资产在各类场景下的运行规律。

We explore correlations with key drivers such as real and nominal GDP growth, inflation, interest rates, bond yields, debt and deficit levels, and more — with the goal of helping investors tilt the odds in their favour.

我们深入研究了资产回报与核心驱动因素的关联性，包括实际及名义 GDP 增长、通胀、利率、债券收益率、债务与赤字水平等 —— 最终目的是帮助投资者提升投资胜率。

Ultimately, this report aims to offer insights that could help you retire a few years earlier, grow a larger pension pot, give your children a better start, or simply outperform your peers over the course of your career.

归根结底，本报告旨在提供实用洞见，助力读者提前数年退休、积累更丰厚的养老金、为子女创造更优起点，或在职业生涯中实现投资表现超越同行。

一如既往，欢迎引用本报告内容。若需引用，敬请注明出处为德意志银行研究所。也欢迎引导他人访问官网（www.dbresearch.com/research-institute）查阅本报告及其他更多研究成果。
